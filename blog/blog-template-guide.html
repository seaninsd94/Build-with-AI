1. Blog Post Structure (Every Post Follows This)

Every blog post on your site should follow this 10-part structure to maximize both SEO rankings and conversions. This structure is designed for Square/Weebly's blog editor but works on any platform.

Part 1: SEO Title Tag

This is what appears in Google search results and browser tabs.

  • Max 60 characters (Google truncates longer titles)
  • Primary keyword first, then brand or location
  • Include "San Diego" when relevant for local SEO
  • Include a number, year, or price when possible (increases CTR)
GOOD: "Tent Rental Sizes Guide | San Diego Events | Tasteful Event Rentals" GOOD: "Event Rental Prices San Diego (2026) | Tables from $9.50" BAD: "Blog Post About Tents - Tasteful Event Rentals Website" BAD: "Everything You Need to Know About Renting Tents for Events"

Part 2: Meta Description

The 1-2 sentence preview that appears below your title in Google results.

  • Max 155 characters
  • Include primary keyword naturally
  • Include a call-to-action ("Book online," "Get a free quote")
  • Include a trust signal (price, rating, or differentiator)
TEMPLATE: "[Answer to search query in 1 sentence]. [Trust signal]. [CTA]. Tasteful Event Rentals, San Diego." EXAMPLE: "Choose from pop-up, marquee & frame tents for any San Diego event. Starting at $95 with free setup. Book online or call (858) 255-1130."

Part 3: Hero Image

  • Use YOUR OWN event photos whenever possible (not stock)
  • Recommended size: 1200x630px (also works for social sharing)
  • Compress to under 200KB using TinyPNG or Squoosh
  • Alt text: Describe what's in the image + include keyword
    Example: "20x20 pop-up tent setup for backyard wedding in San Diego"

Part 4: Introduction (2-3 sentences)

Hook the reader immediately. Address their problem or question directly.

FORMULA: [Acknowledge the reader's situation] + [Preview the answer] + [Why trust us] EXAMPLE: "Planning an outdoor event in San Diego and not sure what size tent you need? This guide breaks down tent sizes by guest count, event type, and layout style. As a 5-star rated rental company that's set up hundreds of San Diego events, we'll help you pick the perfect fit."

Part 5: Body Content

  • 800-1,500 words (sweet spot for SEO and readability)
  • Use H2 headings for major sections (3-5 per post)
  • Use H3 headings for sub-sections
  • Keep paragraphs short (2-4 sentences max)
  • Use bullet points for lists and features
  • Include comparison tables when comparing options
  • Bold key takeaways and important numbers

Part 6: Internal Product Links

CRITICAL: Every blog post MUST link to at least 1-2 of your product/category pages on Booqable. This drives readers from the blog to your actual rental catalog where they can book.
EXAMPLE LINKS TO INCLUDE: - "Browse our full selection of [tent rentals](/tent-rentals-event-rentals-san-diego)" - "View our [chair options starting at $1.95](/chairs-rentals-event-rentals-san-diego)" - "Check out our [table rentals](/table-rentals-event-rentals-san-diego) for your event" - "See our complete [rental catalogue](/rental-catalogue)"

Part 7: Visual Content (Mid-Article)

  • Include 2-4 images throughout the post
  • Use comparison tables for "vs." posts
  • Use numbered lists for checklists
  • Charts or calculators for sizing/quantity posts
  • Every image needs descriptive alt text with keywords

Part 8: CTA Block

Place a prominent call-to-action after the main content. This is what converts readers into customers.

Ready to Book Your Event Rentals?

Chairs from $1.95 | Tables from $9.50 | Tents from $95

Free white-glove delivery & setup in San Diego County

Browse Rentals Call (858) 255-1130

Part 9: Related Posts

Link to 2-3 related blog posts at the bottom. This keeps readers on your site longer (reduces bounce rate) and builds internal link authority.

EXAMPLE: "You might also enjoy:" - How Many Tables and Chairs Do I Need? (Guest Count Calculator) - The Complete Event Rental Checklist - How Much Do Event Rentals Cost in San Diego?

Part 10: FAQ Section (with Schema Markup)

End every post with 2-3 frequently asked questions. These can appear as expandable answers directly in Google search results.

3. SEO Checklist (Use Before Publishing Each Post)

On-Page SEO

  • Title tag is under 60 characters and includes primary keyword
  • Meta description is under 155 characters with CTA
  • URL slug is short, descriptive, includes keyword (e.g., /blog/tent-size-guide-san-diego)
  • H1 heading matches or closely matches the title tag
  • Primary keyword appears in first 100 words
  • H2/H3 subheadings include secondary keywords naturally
  • "San Diego" appears 3-5 times throughout the post
  • Post is 800-1,500 words

Internal Linking

  • Links to at least 1 product/category page (tents, chairs, tables)
  • Links to 2-3 related blog posts
  • Links to contact/booking page
  • Phone number (858) 255-1130 appears at least once

Images

  • Hero image is 1200x630px and under 200KB
  • All images have descriptive alt text with keywords
  • 2-4 images throughout the post
  • Images are your own event photos when possible

Conversion Elements

  • CTA block appears after main content
  • Pricing anchor mentioned (chairs $1.95, tables $9.50, tents $95)
  • Phone number and/or "Book Online" link included
  • FAQ section at the bottom (2-3 questions)
  • FAQ schema markup added via embed code

Before Publishing

  • Proofread for typos and grammar
  • All links work correctly
  • Preview on mobile - does it look good?
  • Share preview image looks correct (use Facebook's Sharing Debugger)
  • Post is assigned to the correct blog category/pillar

4. Writing Style Guide

Voice & Tone

  • Friendly but professional - like talking to a trusted vendor, not a corporate brochure
  • Confident and helpful - you've done hundreds of events, share that expertise
  • Local and personal - mention San Diego neighborhoods, weather, venues by name
  • Action-oriented - always tell the reader what to do next

Dos

  • Use "you" and "your" (talk to the reader directly)
  • Use specific numbers ($1.95, 50 guests, 20x20 tent)
  • Reference real San Diego locations and conditions
  • Include your competitive advantages (5-day rental, white-glove service, pricing)
  • End with a clear next step

Don'ts

  • Don't use jargon without explaining it
  • Don't write walls of text - use short paragraphs, bullets, and tables
  • Don't be pushy or salesy - educate first, sell second
  • Don't use stock photos when you have real event photos
  • Don't forget to link to your rental pages
