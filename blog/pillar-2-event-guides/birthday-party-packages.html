The Birthday Party Rental Essentials

Every birthday party, regardless of age or size, needs three things: shade, seating, and surfaces. In San Diego, where outdoor birthday parties are possible 12 months a year, these basics set the foundation for everything else.

Tents: Your Outdoor Party Headquarters

A pop-up tent does more than block the sun. It creates a defined party space, protects the cake from melting in San Diego heat, gives older guests a comfortable shaded retreat, and serves as a visual anchor that says "the party is here."

  • 10x10 pop-up tent ($95): Perfect for parties of 15-25 guests. Covers the main food and cake area with room for a few chairs.
  • 10x20 pop-up tent: Ideal for 25-50 guests. Enough space for a full party setup including dining, cake, and presents.
  • Marquee/festival tent: For milestone birthdays or big celebrations with 50+ guests. Creates a true event atmosphere.

Tables: More Than Just Eating Surfaces

At a birthday party, tables serve multiple roles:

  • Dining table(s): Round tables seat 8-10 guests each and encourage conversation. Rectangular tables work great for kids who want to sit together in a row.
  • Cake and dessert table: A dedicated table for the birthday cake, cupcakes, or dessert spread. This becomes the photo backdrop for the "blowing out the candles" moment.
  • Gift table: Give guests a place to set presents when they arrive instead of piling them in a corner.
  • Activity table: For kids' parties, a rectangular table works perfectly for crafts, coloring, or party favors.

Folding chairs at $1.95 are the go-to for birthday parties. They are clean, sturdy, and affordable enough to rent in quantity. For a 40-guest party, your entire seating cost is under $80. Birthday Party Packages by Age

The best party setup depends on who is celebrating. Here is what works for each age group:

Kids' Birthday Parties (Ages 1-12)

Kids' parties are all about fun, movement, and keeping tiny humans entertained. The essentials:

  • 1 x 10x10 or 10x20 pop-up tent for shade and food
  • 2-3 rectangular tables (eating, cake, crafts)
  • 15-25 folding chairs (kids and parents)
  • 2+ lawn games (cornhole, giant Jenga, Connect Four)
  • Optional: photobooth with silly props

Pro tip: For kids under 6, focus more on games and less on formal seating. Kids will not sit still anyway. For ages 7-12, add a photobooth, because pre-teens love it.

Teen Birthday Parties (Ages 13-19)

Teens want their party to feel cool, not childish. Think casual and social:

  • 1 x 10x20 pop-up tent or larger for a hangout zone
  • 3-4 round tables for clusters of friends
  • 20-30 chairs arranged in social groupings
  • Lawn games (competitive games are a big hit)
  • Photobooth (essential for teens and social media)

Pro tip: Set up the photobooth with trending props. Teens will use it all party long and share photos instantly.

Adult Birthday Parties (21-49)

Adult birthdays range from casual backyard BBQs to themed dinner parties. A flexible setup works best:

  • 1-2 pop-up tents or a festival tent depending on size
  • Round tables with linens for sit-down dining
  • 30-60 chairs (padded resin for a classier look)
  • Cocktail/high-top tables if serving drinks
  • Optional: games, photobooth, dance area

Pro tip: Add linens in a color theme. White tablecloths with a colored runner instantly elevate a backyard table into something special.

Milestone Birthdays (30th, 40th, 50th, 60th+)

Milestone birthdays deserve milestone setups. Go bigger and more polished:

  • Marquee or festival tent for the wow factor
  • 8-15 round tables with premium linens
  • 60-100+ padded resin chairs
  • Head table for the guest of honor
  • Photobooth with milestone-themed props
  • Lawn games for a relaxed cocktail hour

Pro tip: A milestone birthday with a tent, round tables, elegant linens, and padded chairs feels like a wedding reception at a fraction of the cost. Your guest of honor will feel incredibly celebrated.

The Best Add-Ons for Birthday Parties

Lawn Games

Lawn games are the easiest way to take a birthday party from good to great. They fill the gaps between food, cake, and presents with active, social fun. The most popular options:

  • Cornhole: Works for literally every age group. Set up a casual tournament or just let people play throughout the party.
  • Giant Jenga: The suspense of pulling that last block is pure entertainment for spectators and players alike.
  • Giant Connect Four: Colorful, easy to understand, and perfect for younger guests.

Photobooth

A photobooth pays for itself in memories. Guests get instant photo keepsakes, the birthday person gets a collection of fun shots with everyone who attended, and it serves as entertainment that requires zero planning or supervision. Set it up under a tent for the best lighting.

Linens

Tablecloths, runners, and napkins from our rental catalogue are the simplest upgrade you can make. Plain rental tables look like, well, rental tables. Add a linen and suddenly you have a tablescape that coordinates with your party theme.
