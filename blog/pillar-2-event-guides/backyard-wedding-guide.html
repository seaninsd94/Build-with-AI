Why San Diego Is Perfect for Backyard Weddings

With an average of 266 sunny days per year and summer temperatures hovering between 72 and 80 degrees, San Diego delivers the kind of weather that event planners dream about. Communities across the county, from La Mesa and El Cajon to Chula Vista and Encinitas, offer spacious residential lots with mature landscaping that serves as a natural backdrop.

A backyard wedding also gives you complete control over your timeline, vendor choices, and guest count, all while saving thousands compared to a traditional venue. The trade-off is that you need to bring in your own infrastructure, and that is where event rentals become essential.

Tent Selection for San Diego Weather

Even in San Diego, a tent is one of the most important investments for your backyard wedding. Here is why and which type to choose:

Pop-Up Tents (10x10 and 10x20)

These are best suited for smaller roles at a wedding: shading a beverage station, covering a DJ setup, or creating a shaded area for elderly guests. A 10x10 pop-up tent starts at just $95 and is quick to set up. Consider placing one or two near your ceremony area for guests who need relief from the sun.

Marquee and Festival Tents

For your main reception area, a marquee or festival tent is the way to go. These larger structures provide an elegant canopy that defines your celebration space, supports string lighting and drapery, and protects against San Diego's occasional June gloom or rare winter drizzle. Plan for approximately 15 to 20 square feet per guest when calculating tent size, and add extra footage for the dance floor and head table.

San Diego Tip: Even in summer, coastal neighborhoods like Pacific Beach and Point Loma can experience marine layer fog until mid-morning. If your ceremony starts before 11 a.m., a tent keeps your setup dry from morning dew and provides a comfortable gathering point.

Linens, Decor, and the Finishing Touches

Tables without linens look like a corporate meeting. With them, you have a wedding. Our linen collection includes tablecloths and overlays in a range of colors that complement any wedding palette. White and ivory are timeless choices, while sage green, dusty rose, or navy can add depth to your tablescape.

Consider these finishing touches:

  • Table runners over solid linens for a layered, textured look
  • Napkins in a contrasting or complementary color
  • Chair sashes to tie into your color scheme

Dance Floor Considerations

Most backyards have grass, which is not ideal for dancing. If your budget allows, renting a portable dance floor is a worthwhile investment. A 12x12 dance floor works for 50 to 80 guests, while a 15x15 or larger floor suits bigger receptions.

If a dance floor is not in the budget, consider these alternatives:

  • Choose a home with a patio or deck area that can double as a dance space
  • Lay plywood covered with outdoor carpet or vinyl for a DIY approach
  • Embrace lawn dancing and advise guests to wear wedges or flats instead of heels

Your Backyard Wedding Rental Timeline

Timing is everything, especially in San Diego where peak wedding season runs from May through October. Follow this timeline to secure the best availability:

  • 3 months before: Book your tent, tables, chairs, and linens. This is especially critical for Saturday weddings in summer.
  • 2 months before: Finalize your layout and confirm quantities. Walk the backyard with measurements and share them with your rental company.
  • 1 month before: Confirm delivery time and setup logistics. Discuss access points, gate widths, and any slopes in the yard.
  • 1 week before: Final confirmation call. Ensure your lawn is mowed and the delivery area is clear.

Final Tips for a Flawless Backyard Wedding

  • Check with your HOA or city: Some La Mesa, San Diego, and surrounding neighborhoods have noise ordinances or event restrictions. Verify before you plan.
  • Plan for restrooms: If your guest count exceeds what your home restrooms can handle, look into portable restroom rentals.
  • Power supply: Make sure your home's electrical panel can support lighting, sound, and catering equipment. You may need a generator.
  • Parking: Arrange a shuttle or valet service if street parking is limited.
  • Have a backup plan: A tent is your built-in backup for San Diego's rare rainy days, especially during the winter months of December through February.

How far in advance should I book wedding rentals in San Diego?

Do I need a tent for a backyard wedding in San Diego?
