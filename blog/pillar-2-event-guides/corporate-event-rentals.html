San Diego ranks as one of the best cities in the country for outdoor events, and corporate planners are taking notice. Tent Options for Outdoor Corporate Events

A tent instantly elevates any outdoor space from "informal gathering" to "planned professional event." Here are the best options for corporate settings:

Pop-Up Tents (10x10 and 10x20)

Starting at just $95, pop-up tents are ideal for smaller corporate events. Use them for:

  • Registration and check-in stations
  • Catering and beverage service areas
  • Branded activation spaces (add company banners to the tent frame)
  • Shaded seating areas for networking breakout groups

A 10x20 pop-up provides enough covered space for a food service area with seating for 15-20 people, or a standing networking area for 25-30.

Marquee and Festival Tents

For larger corporate events of 50 to 200 attendees, a marquee or festival tent provides the scale and elegance your event demands. These tents create a true event venue feel, complete with enough height for presentations, stage setups, and even projection screens. They also convey professionalism, showing your clients and employees that this event was planned with care.

Branding Tip: White pop-up tents are a blank canvas for your company's branding. Hang banners from the frame, place branded table runners across your registration table, and consider custom signage on easels at the tent entrance. The clean white backdrop of the tent makes company colors pop in photos.

Networking-Friendly Layouts

The biggest mistake at corporate events is creating a layout that traps people at their tables. For networking events and mixers, use these strategies:

  • Use fewer chairs than guests. This keeps people moving and mingling rather than sitting in one spot.
  • Create conversation zones. Place small clusters of 3-4 chairs with a side table in different areas of the tent. This invites brief, focused conversations.
  • Keep food stations mobile. Multiple small food stations spread around the space pull people in different directions, naturally creating new networking opportunities.
  • Leave open space. Do not fill every square foot. Open floor area is where spontaneous conversations happen.

Add a Photobooth for Engagement

A photobooth is not just for weddings and birthdays. Corporate event planners increasingly add photobooths because they drive social media engagement, create branded content when you add a custom frame or backdrop, give attendees a tangible takeaway, and serve as a natural icebreaker at networking events.

Games for Team Building

If your corporate event includes a team-building component, lawn games are an easy, low-pressure way to get people interacting. Cornhole tournaments, giant Jenga challenges, and oversized Connect Four are all popular at San Diego corporate events. They work for groups of any size and require no special skills or preparation.
