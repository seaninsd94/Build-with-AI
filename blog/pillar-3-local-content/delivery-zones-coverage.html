Desert Delivery Considerations

Palm Springs is approximately 2 to 2.5 hours from our La Mesa warehouse via I-8 and Highway 86 or I-15 and I-10. For desert events, we plan deliveries carefully:

  • Delivery timing: We schedule desert deliveries for early morning or late afternoon to avoid the midday heat, which can affect equipment during transport
  • 5-day rental period: Especially helpful for desert events, giving us flexibility to deliver a day or two early and pick up a day or two after
  • Heat considerations: We advise on tent and shade solutions specific to desert conditions, where temperatures can exceed 100 degrees in summer
  • Minimum order: Desert deliveries require a minimum order size. Contact us for details.

Peak Desert Season

The desert event season runs roughly opposite to San Diego's peak season. October through April is prime time for Palm Springs events, with comfortable temperatures in the 70s and 80s. This is when we see the most demand for desert deliveries, especially around the winter holidays, Coachella festival season (April), and Stagecoach (also April).

How the 5-Day Rental Period Works With Delivery

Our 5-day rental period is one of the most generous in the event rental industry, and it is designed to work hand-in-hand with our delivery service. Here is how it typically breaks down:

  • Day 1: Delivery and setup (we bring everything and set it up at your venue)
  • Day 2: Buffer day for final preparations, decorating, and walkthrough
  • Day 3: Your event day
  • Day 4: Rest and recovery day (no rush to break down)
  • Day 5: Pickup (we come collect everything)

This schedule is flexible. For local San Diego events, we can deliver morning-of and pick up the next day. For distant deliveries to LA or Palm Springs, the 5-day window ensures we can schedule efficient delivery routes without putting time pressure on you.

Booking Delivery: What We Need From You

When you book your rentals, here is the information that helps us plan your delivery:

  • Event address: Full street address or venue name
  • Event date: So we can check availability and schedule delivery
  • Access information: Gate codes, parking restrictions, elevator access, stairs, or narrow pathways we should know about
  • Setup location: Where on the property you want everything set up (backyard, front lawn, park pavilion, etc.)
  • Contact person: Who our delivery team should coordinate with on-site
