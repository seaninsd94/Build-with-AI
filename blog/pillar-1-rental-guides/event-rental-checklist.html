The Complete Event Rental Checklist: Everything You Need to Rent

Tasteful Event Rentals • Updated April 2026 • 10 min read

Planning an event means juggling dozens of details, and forgetting one rental item can create a headache on the big day. This comprehensive checklist covers every category of event rentals — from tents and tables to games and photo booths — so you can plan with confidence and avoid last-minute scrambles.

Use this list as your master planning document. Not every event needs every item, but scanning the full list ensures you won't overlook something important. We've organized it by category and added notes on what matters most for San Diego outdoor events.

How to Use This Checklist

  1. Start with your guest count. This drives quantities for every category. Use our guest count calculator for exact table and chair numbers.
  2. Identify your venue type. Indoor venues provide most infrastructure. Outdoor/backyard events need more rentals (tents, lighting, power).
  3. Check each category below. Mark what you need and note quantities.
  4. Book early. San Diego's peak event season (May–October) fills up fast. Reserve 4–8 weeks ahead for the best selection.

The Complete Checklist

Shelter and Weather Protection

  • Pop-up tent (10x10 or 10x20) — shade stations, vendor booths, bar/food coverage
  • Marquee / festival tent — main event coverage for 50+ guests
  • Frame tent — for hard surfaces (patios, driveways, parking lots)
  • Tent sidewalls — wind protection, privacy, evening warmth
  • Tent gutters — connect multiple tents to channel rainwater
  • Tent fans — essential for summer events in inland San Diego (La Mesa, El Cajon, Santee)

See our tent size guide and tent types comparison for help choosing.

Seating

  • Folding chairs — ceremonies, casual events, budget-friendly (from $1.95 each)
  • Padded resin chairs — receptions, formal dinners, long events
  • Specialty chairs (Chiavari, cross-back) — upscale weddings, galas
  • Bar stools / high-top chairs — cocktail areas
  • Lounge furniture — chill-out zones, cocktail hours
  • Benches — ceremony seating, rustic events
  • Kids' chairs — children's tables at family events
  • Chair cushions or covers — dress up standard chairs

Compare options: Folding vs. padded resin chairs

Tables

  • 60" round tables — seat 8, standard for receptions (from $9.50)
  • 48" round tables — seat 6, cocktail groupings, smaller events
  • 6 ft rectangular / banquet tables — seat 6, family-style dining
  • 8 ft rectangular / banquet tables — seat 8–10, banquets, long table setups
  • Cocktail / high-top tables — standing areas, cocktail hours, bars
  • Kids' tables — lower height for children's seating
  • Registration / check-in table — 6 ft rectangular with skirting
  • Gift table — 4–6 ft, positioned near entrance
  • Cake / dessert table — 4–6 ft with decorative linen
  • DJ / AV table — 6–8 ft with power access

Layout guide: Round vs. banquet tables

Linens and Table Decor

  • Tablecloths — matched to table size and shape (round or rectangular)
  • Table runners — accent color over neutral cloth
  • Cloth napkins — matching or contrasting with tablecloths
  • Chair covers — transform standard folding chairs
  • Chair sashes or ties — accent color, tied at back
  • Table skirting — for registration, buffet, cake, and gift tables
  • Overlays — decorative layer over base tablecloth

Browse linen options in our rental catalogue.

Food and Beverage Service

  • Buffet tables — 6–8 ft with skirting
  • Chafing dishes / food warmers — keep hot food at safe temperature
  • Serving platters and bowls — if caterer doesn't provide
  • Drink dispensers / beverage coolers — water, lemonade, punch
  • Bar table — high-top or standard with bar skirting
  • Ice bins / coolers — for bottled drinks or icing down kegs
  • Trash and recycling bins — often overlooked but essential
  • Hand-washing station — required for food service at many San Diego parks

Entertainment and Activities

  • Photo booth — backdrop, props, lighting
  • Lawn games — cornhole, giant Jenga, ring toss, ladder golf
  • Dance floor — portable panels for grass or uneven surfaces
  • Stage or platform — for bands, DJs, speakers
  • Audio system — speakers, microphone, mixer (if venue doesn't provide)
  • Projector and screen — slideshows, presentations, movie nights
  • Kids' activities — bounce house, face painting station, craft table

Tasteful Event Rentals offers photo booth and game rentals — great add-ons for any event.

Lighting

  • String lights / bistro lights — warm ambiance for evening events
  • Uplighting — color wash on tent poles, walls, or trees
  • Spotlights — highlight cake table, centerpieces, head table
  • Pathway lighting — luminaries or stakes along walkways
  • Tent globe lights — interior tent illumination
  • Candelabras or lanterns — table-level ambiance

San Diego sunsets around 7–8 PM in summer, 5 PM in winter. Plan lighting for events that extend past sunset.

Miscellaneous and Often Forgotten

  • Extension cords and power strips — for DJ, lighting, food warmers
  • Generator — if venue has no power access (common at parks and beaches)
  • Portable restrooms — for remote venues or 100+ guests
  • Signage and easels — welcome sign, seating chart, menu board
  • Coat rack — for cooler-weather evening events
  • Stanchions and ropes — crowd management for large events
  • Heaters (patio style) — evening events October through March
  • Fans or misting stations — summer daytime events inland
  • Weights / sandbags — tent anchoring on hard surfaces or sandy soil
  • First aid kit — always smart to have on site
San Diego park tip: If your event is at a City of San Diego park (Balboa Park, Mission Bay, Presidio Park), you'll need a park use permit. Many parks have rules about tent stakes (no ground penetration on some surfaces) and require trash removal. Check with the Parks & Recreation department at least 4 weeks before your event.

Checklist by Event Type

Not sure which categories apply? Here's a quick guide by event type:

Backyard Birthday Party (25–50 guests)

  • 1–2 pop-up tents for shade
  • 4–8 tables (mix of dining and activity tables)
  • 25–50 folding chairs
  • Tablecloths in party colors
  • Lawn games
  • Photo booth (optional)

Wedding Reception (75–150 guests)

  • Large tent (20x40 or 30x30+) with sidewalls
  • Round tables for dining + 1–2 rectangular for head table
  • Padded resin or Chiavari chairs
  • Full linen package (tablecloths, napkins, runners)
  • Dance floor
  • String lights + uplighting
  • Bar setup (high-top table, skirting)
  • Cake table, gift table, guest book table
  • Photo booth
  • Heaters (for evening events)

Corporate Event or Fundraiser (50–200 guests)

  • Tent or indoor venue setup
  • Mix of round and banquet tables
  • Padded resin chairs
  • Registration table with skirting
  • Stage or riser
  • Audio system and podium
  • Projector and screen
  • Cocktail tables for networking areas
  • Signage and easels

Casual Outdoor Party (20–40 guests)

  • 1 pop-up tent for food/drink station
  • 2–4 tables
  • 20–40 folding chairs
  • Cooler or drink dispenser
  • Lawn games
  • Trash/recycling bins

Booking Timeline for San Diego Events

Here's when to finalize your rentals based on the season:

  • 8+ weeks before: Peak season weddings (May–October), holiday weekends (July 4th, Labor Day, Memorial Day), graduation parties (May–June).
  • 4–6 weeks before: Standard peak season events, large corporate gatherings, community festivals.
  • 2–3 weeks before: Off-season events (November–March), smaller gatherings under 50 guests.
  • 1 week before: Last-minute additions are sometimes possible depending on availability — call to ask.

Frequently Asked Questions

How far in advance should I book event rentals?

For peak season events in San Diego (May through October), book at least 4–6 weeks in advance. For weddings, 2–3 months is ideal. Holiday weekends and graduation season (May–June) are the busiest — book those 6–8 weeks out. Off-season events (November through March) can often be booked 2–3 weeks ahead.

What's included in an event rental delivery?

At Tasteful Event Rentals, delivery includes drop-off, setup, and pickup within a 5-day rental window. Tables and chairs are placed according to your layout. Tents are fully assembled and anchored. You don't need to fold, stack, or disassemble anything — our crew handles all of it.

Do I need to rent linens separately from tables?

Yes, linens are typically rented separately. We recommend tablecloths for any formal event — they transform basic rental tables into elegant surfaces. For casual events like backyard BBQs, you can skip linens entirely. Check our rental catalogue for available linen colors and sizes.
